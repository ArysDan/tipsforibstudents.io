Tips for sleep problems

Avoiding lack of sleep is crucial for overall well-being. Students suffer from this because of the stress, academic workload and late-night studying. Quality sleep restores the body and mind, supporting physical and mental health. Chronic sleep leads to increased risk of hypertension, diabetes, obesity, depression, heart attack, and stroke (Institute of Medicine, 2006)

Tips:

Establish a Regular Sleep Schedule: Try to go to bed and wake up at the same time every day, even on weekends. This helps regulate your body's internal clock and improves the quality of your sleep (TED, 2020)
Create a Relaxing Bedtime Routine: Wind down before bed by doing calming activities like reading a book, taking a warm bath, or listening to soothing music (TED, 2020)
Create a Comfortable Sleep Environment: Make your sleeping space comfortable and inviting. Keep your bedroom dark, quiet, and at a cool, comfortable temperature. A good mattress and pillows can make a big difference in the quality of your sleep (TED, 2020)
Limit Screen Time Before Bed: The blue light from screens can interfere with your body's production of melatonin, a hormone that helps you sleep (Salamon, 2022). Try to avoid phones, tablets, and computers at least an hour before bedtime
