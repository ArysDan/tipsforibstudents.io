Tips for time management

Time management is a critical life skill. Effective time management enhances productivity and reduces stress, while poor time management leads to the load of duties. It enables a balanced life, with time for study, and personal interests. According to the conducted survey students experience lack of time

There are such tips and techniques as: Pomodoro Technique, Eisenhower Matrix, Avoid Procrastination, Time Blocking

Break your study time into 25-minute chunks. Work for 25 minutes, then take a 5-minute break. After four chunks, take a longer 15-30 minute break. This helps you stay focused

Plan your day by dividing it into time blocks. Assign each block to a specific task or activity. Stick to your schedule to make the most of your time. For example, you can have a block for studying, a block for exercise, and a block for relaxation

- Urgent and Important: Do these first

- Important but Not Urgent: Schedule them

- Urgent but Not Important: Consider delegating

- Not Urgent and Not Important: Think about eliminating or postponing (Stojanovic, 2023)

Procrastination can be a big time-waster. Try to start your tasks without delaying them. Break big tasks into smaller, manageable steps. Reward yourself when you complete a task to stay motivated
