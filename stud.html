Tips for studying

Effective study habits favor critical thinking and problem-solving abilities. They help students grasp complex concepts and retain information, leading to better grades and greater confidence and unlock their full potential to achieve their goals

Dedicated Study Space

Find a quiet and comfortable place to study, free from distractions like your phone or TV. This space should associate with studying to help you stay focused

SQ3R Technique

Start by surveying the material to get an overview; Create questions about what you need to learn; Read the material actively, looking for answers to your questions; Recite or explain what you've learned in your own words; Review the material periodically to reinforce your understanding (Nebel, 2021)

Set Realistic Goals

Break your study sessions into smaller, manageable goals. For example, aim to complete a specific chapter or topic in a set amount of time. Achieving these mini-goals keeps you motivated

Feynman Technique

Choose a concept you are studying. Explain it as if you were teaching it to someone else and use simple language. Identify gaps in your understanding while explaining to understand the topic more
